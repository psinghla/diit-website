01 — Brand architecture

One institute, two voices

The biggest fix from v1: stop dressing the whole institute as an AI startup. The eleven NIELIT, Tally and programming courses — the ones parents and government-job aspirants pay for — wear the calm Master brand. Only the new AI courses get the techy AI sub-brand. Same logo, same teal, same rules; the AI look is a controlled accent, not the default.

05 — Discipline

The rules that keep it one brand

v1's worry was that the logo and the course deck looked like two companies. These rules — plus the Master/sub-brand split — force everything onto one identity.

Always

  • Lead with teal — logo, headers, primary buttons, links.
  • Use navy only as a dark background for heroes, footers and ad panels.
  • Limit vermilion to one CTA per screen ("Enroll Now", "Book a seat").
  • Keep NIELIT + DIIT logos paired in the top corner for credibility.
  • Set Master headlines in Sora, body in Inter, Hindi in Mukta — every time.
  • Keep Space Grotesk, JetBrains Mono and Code Teal inside AI courses only.

Never

  • Run vermilion and amber loudly together — that's the webinar-funnel look.
  • Put mono fonts or Code Teal on CCC, O Level, Tally or institute material.
  • Add gradients, drop shadows, or a new colour "to make it pop".
  • Mix more than two fonts in a single piece (Mukta for Hindi doesn't count).
  • Stretch, recolour, rotate or add a glow to the logo.
  • Use the old coral #F23E5C anywhere — it is fully retired.
